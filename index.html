Background

Who I Am

I am a Data and M&E Specialist with over six years embedded in PEPFAR- and USAID-funded HIV/AIDS and TB programs at ECEWS in Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria. My work sits at the intersection of program accountability and data science — designing indicator frameworks, managing electronic health records, and producing the analytic reports that inform donor decisions.

What drives me is the gap between data collected and decisions made. I have spent my career closing that gap: building dashboards that Program Directors actually use, identifying coverage failures before they become audit findings, and training facility-level staff to understand their own numbers.

My research interests have grown out of this work. Nigeria's HIV response is aging — a generation of patients started on ART in the 2000s now faces chronic comorbidities alongside older adults who were never part of the original epidemic. I am building a research agenda around multimorbidity, aging, and chronic disease management in sub-Saharan Africa, bridging my M&E expertise with gerontological inquiry.

HIV/TB Co-infection Client Tracking System

ECEWS / PEPFAR — Akwa Ibom State

Designed and built a dedicated tracking system to identify and monitor HIV-positive clients who were also TB-positive — closing a critical gap where co-infected patients were falling through between siloed HIV and TB registers. The tracker consolidated patient data across both programmes and flagged high-priority clients for follow-up.

Achieved 80% treatment outcome rate among tracked HIV/TB co-infected clients
Enabled systematic identification of co-infected clients previously missed by routine reporting
Improved coordination between HIV and TB programme teams at facility level
